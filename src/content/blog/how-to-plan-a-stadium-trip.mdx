"Groundhopping" — travelling to tick off stadiums and matches — is one of the
most rewarding niche trips in sport. It just needs planning around fixture
lists that are not confirmed until weeks before.

## Step 1: Decide matches or museums

Two trips in one hobby: attending live fixtures (atmosphere, but rigid dates
and ticket scarcity) or stadium tours and museums (flexible, year-round, but no
crowd). Many great trips mix both — one big match anchored by tour visits
around it. Decide the balance first.

## Step 2: Build around the fixture list

This is the groundhopper's core problem: leagues confirm exact kickoff
dates and times only weeks ahead (TV moves them). Plan a target weekend and a
region, hold flexible travel, and finalise the exact match schedule once
fixtures and broadcast picks are released.

## Step 3: Cluster geographically

Open the [map](/map), filter to a city or region, and find stadiums close
enough to chain. A city with several clubs (London, Madrid, Buenos Aires,
Moscow) can yield a tour in the day and a match at night. Tight clusters make
multi-ground weekends possible.

## Step 4: Sort tickets by difficulty

Rank fixtures by how hard tickets are: derbies and big clubs need memberships,
pre-sales, or hospitality booked far ahead; mid-table and lower-league games
are easy and often walk-up. Build the trip's spine around the hard ticket and
fill with easy ones.

## Step 5: Use stadium tours as the reliable layer

Tours of famous grounds run most days year-round, are bookable in advance, and
guarantee access regardless of the fixture chaos. They are the dependable
backbone of a stadium trip when matches cannot be locked.

## Step 6: Plan the matchday logistics

For each live game: official tickets only, bag policy checked, ID if required,
transport out pre-planned (egress is the worst part), and time built in for the
pre-match build-up, which is half the experience.

## Step 7: Pace the trip

Two stadiums a day maximum if one is a match; tours are quicker. Build a rest
or sightseeing block — stadium trips are long days of standing and travel.

## Step 8: Respect local fan culture

Wear neutral or home colours in home areas, follow singing-end norms, keep
rivalry friendly, and obey stewards. Groundhoppers are guests in other fans'
cathedrals.

## Put it together

Matches or museums, then a target region, then fixtures, then tickets by
difficulty, with tours as the safety net. Open the [map](/map), cluster the
grounds, and a stadium trip becomes a brilliantly structured one.
