The Estadio Alberto J. Armando, universally known as La Bombonera, is the home of Club Atlético Boca Juniors and the most architecturally distinctive football ground in South America. Three of its four sides rise as steep vertical tiers; the fourth, restricted by the line of the adjacent street, is a flat single-storey stand topped by VIP boxes. The asymmetry gives the ground its nickname — bombonera means "chocolate box" — and the configuration is widely credited with the celebrated acoustic effect of "the stadium that trembles". ## Origins (1938-1940)

La Bombonera was built between 1938 and 1940 in the working-class barrio of La Boca in the Buenos Aires port district, on the site of an earlier Boca Juniors ground. The design, by the Slovenian-Argentine architect Viktor Sulčič with engineer José Luis Delpini, was constrained by the narrow city block on which it sits — a constraint that produced the asymmetric three-tier-plus-flat-side configuration that has defined the stadium ever since. The inaugural match on 25 May 1940 was Boca against San Lorenzo (won 2-0 by Boca).

## The asymmetric design

The three vertical tiers on the south, north, and east sides rise sharply, placing supporters extremely close to and directly above the pitch. The west side — adjacent to Brandsen Street — could not be extended to match, and so remains a single-storey stand topped by hospitality boxes. The asymmetry has been preserved through every subsequent renovation; multiple proposals to demolish the west stand and complete the bowl have been resisted, with the unusual silhouette now treated as part of the ground's heritage.

## La Doce and the trembling stadium

The Boca supporter group La Doce — "the twelfth man" — occupies the south end of the stadium and is one of the largest and most-organised supporter organisations in Argentine football. The combination of the close, steep tiers and the concrete construction produces a documented vertical resonance under coordinated jumping: the stadium literally moves. Visiting players have described the sensation as feeling vibration through the pitch, and engineering studies have measured small but real displacement of the upper stands during peak supporter activity.

## The Superclásico

The Superclásico between Boca Juniors and River Plate is one of football's most-followed fixtures, regularly described by international polls as among the world's most intense sporting rivalries. La Bombonera hosts the Boca-home leg, which produces atmospheres that have entered the cultural mythology of South American football. River's home leg at the Monumental offers a different visual character — a fully circular bowl — but the Bombonera leg, with the asymmetric three-tier wall of supporters and the trembling vibration, is the more architecturally distinctive.

## Maradona at Boca

Diego Maradona played two spells at Boca Juniors (1981-1982 and 1995-1997), bookending his career at his boyhood club. The palco — the corner box from which Maradona watched matches in his later years — has been preserved as the Maradona memorial section since his death in 2020. The Bombonera was the principal venue for the public mourning that followed his death, and his image is now a permanent presence both inside and outside the stadium.

## The Copa Libertadores

La Bombonera has been the home venue of Boca's six Copa Libertadores triumphs (1977, 1978, 2000, 2001, 2003, 2007) and the launching point for many more knock-out runs. The two-legged Libertadores final format means that home legs at the Bombonera have produced some of the most-discussed nights in continental club football — the close walls, vertical tiers, and home-end choreographies converting the ground into a sustained pressure environment unlike anywhere else.

## Capacity and the future

Current capacity is approximately 54,000, modest by global standards but optimised for atmosphere over scale. Boca has periodically discussed building a larger replacement stadium elsewhere in Buenos Aires, but the cultural primacy of La Bombonera in the city's identity has made every proposal politically difficult. As of 2026 the project remains discussed but unrealised, with the Bombonera continuing to host Boca's home matches in its original asymmetric configuration.

## Visiting

La Bombonera offers stadium tours that include the dressing rooms, the player tunnel, the museum (with cabinets dedicated to the Libertadores triumphs and to Maradona), and access to the south end at La Doce's section level. The ground is reached on foot or by bus from the Plaza de Mayo through the colourful streets of La Boca, alongside the Caminito tourist promenade and the brightly painted houses of the historic port district.
