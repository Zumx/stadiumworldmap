Thai football has invested significantly in stadium infrastructure since the early 2000s, driven by the Thai League's expansion and by ambitious provincial clubs. Buriram United's transformation of provincial football and Bangkok's collection of larger venues have given Thailand the strongest Southeast Asian stadium network. Every stadium below is on the [map](/map).

## 1. Rajamangala National Stadium, Bangkok

Capacity: approximately 49,722. Thailand's national stadium, built for the 1998 Asian Games. The Rajamangala hosts the Thailand national team and major Thai FA Cup finals, alongside concerts and athletics. The athletics-track configuration places spectators farther from the pitch than at the dedicated football grounds.

## 2. Chang Arena (Thunder Castle), Buriram

Capacity: 32,600. Buriram United's purpose-built home in the northeastern province of Buriram, opened in 2011. The enclosed bowl is among the best-designed football-specific stadiums in Southeast Asia, and Buriram United's dominance of the Thai League under owner Newin Chidchob made the ground the most consistently successful provincial Thai venue.

## 3. SCG Stadium, Nonthaburi

Capacity: 15,000. Muangthong United's purpose-built home in the northern Bangkok metropolitan area. Opened in 2009, the ground was one of the first modern football-specific venues in Thai club football and has hosted AFC Champions League fixtures.

## 4. Thephasadin Stadium, Bangkok

Capacity: 35,000. An older Bangkok athletics stadium that has been used by various Thai clubs and for AFC fixtures. The ground predates the modern Thai stadium-construction era and reflects an earlier model of stadium provision.

## 5. Suphanburi Stadium

Capacity: approximately 25,000. Suphanburi FC's provincial home in central Thailand. The ground has been progressively modernised and represents the spread of organised top-flight football to Thailand's provincial cities.

## 6. Chonburi Stadium

Capacity: 8,680. Chonburi FC's home in the eastern Gulf of Thailand province. Chonburi were Thai League champions in 2007 and remain one of the most consistent Thai clubs at the top level. The compact ground generates a good atmosphere for its size.

## 7. Leo Stadium, Pathum Thani

Capacity: 12,000. BG Pathum United's home in the Bangkok metropolitan suburb of Pathum Thani. BG Pathum's emergence as Thai League champions in 2020-21 marked a new wave of Thai club football funded by the Boon Rawd Brewery (Singha beer) corporate group.

## 8. Thammasat Stadium, Pathum Thani

Capacity: 25,000. The university stadium at Thammasat in Pathum Thani has hosted Thai national team fixtures, AFC Champions League matches, and university competition. Built for the 1998 Asian Games as a secondary venue.

## 9. PAT Stadium, Bangkok

Capacity: 7,500. Port FC's home in the Khlong Toei port district of Bangkok. The compact urban setting and the connection to the Bangkok dockworker tradition gives Port FC supporters a specific working-class identity in Thai football.

## 10. Mitr Phol Stadium, Ratchaburi

Capacity: 10,000. Ratchaburi Mitr Phol FC's home in the western province of Ratchaburi. The ground reflects the Thai sugar industry's involvement in football sponsorship — Mitr Phol is one of Thailand's largest sugar producers.

## The Thai League's development

The Thai League (Thai T1 League since 2017) operates as Southeast Asia's strongest domestic football competition. The league has 16 clubs and runs from August to May. Buriram United, Muangthong United, BG Pathum United, and Bangkok United have been the most consistent top contenders. Thai clubs have reached AFC Champions League knockout rounds, with Buriram and Muangthong producing the strongest international performances.

## The Thailand national team

Thailand's national team — the War Elephants — has been Southeast Asia's most decorated nation, winning the AFF Championship multiple times. World Cup qualification remains the structural target, with the 2026 expanded format providing the most realistic opportunity in modern times. The Rajamangala Stadium hosts the home qualifying matches.

## Visiting Thai grounds

Bangkok's grounds are spread across the metropolitan area but accessible by BTS Skytrain and MRT subway, with some requiring taxi connections for the final segment. Buriram's Chang Arena requires a flight from Bangkok (about an hour) plus a short drive. The Thai League season runs through the rainy and tourist seasons. Tickets are widely available and matchday atmosphere is friendly toward visiting supporters. All grounds are on the [map](/map).
