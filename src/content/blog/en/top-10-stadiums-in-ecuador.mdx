Ecuadorian stadium culture is split between the coast and the highlands. Quito's 2,800-metre altitude makes the city a fortress for the national team and for LDU Quito; Guayaquil's coastal grounds host Barcelona SC and Emelec in front of South America's most consistently large club crowds. The Clásico del Astillero between Barcelona and Emelec routinely fills the Monumental at over 50,000. Every stadium below is on the [map](/map).

## 1. Estadio Monumental Banco Pichincha, Guayaquil

Capacity: 57,267. Barcelona SC's home in Guayaquil's Banco Pichincha district, the largest stadium in Ecuador. The colossal coastal arena has hosted Copa Libertadores knockout matches and major Ecuadorian national team fixtures. Barcelona's enormous supporter base — known as the Idolo del Astillero — produces one of South America's most consistent club football atmospheres.

## 2. Estadio Casa Blanca (Estadio Rodrigo Paz Delgado), Quito

Capacity: 41,575. LDU Quito's home in northern Quito, at 2,850 metres altitude. The high-altitude conditions create a documented competitive advantage that has anchored LDU's two Copa Libertadores titles (2008 and runner-up in 2008-09 Copa Sudamericana). Visiting teams from the coast or from lower-altitude leagues regularly cite Casa Blanca as one of South America's toughest away venues.

## 3. Estadio Olímpico Atahualpa, Quito

Capacity: 35,742. Quito's principal multipurpose stadium, named after the last Inca emperor. The ground hosts the Ecuador national team's biggest matches, with the altitude playing the same role for the Tricolor as it does for LDU. Liga Pro fixtures, plus athletics meetings, are also held here.

## 4. Estadio George Capwell, Guayaquil

Capacity: 40,000. Emelec's home in Guayaquil, named after the American industrialist who founded the club in 1929. The recently renovated stadium hosts the Clásico del Astillero against Barcelona — one of South America's most-watched derbies — and Copa Libertadores knockout fixtures.

## 5. Estadio Bellavista, Ambato

Capacity: 18,000. Macará Ambato's home in central Ecuador. Ambato's setting in the highlands at over 2,500 metres adds a competitive dimension similar to that of Quito's grounds.

## 6. Estadio Christian Benítez Betancourt, Ibarra

Capacity: 25,000. The Imbabura province's principal football ground, named after the Ecuadorian striker who died of a heart attack in 2013 while playing for El Jaish in Qatar. The stadium reflects the country's continuing recognition of the legacy of Christian Benítez.

## 7. Estadio Reales Tamarindos, Portoviejo

Capacity: 25,000. Portoviejo's coastal ground in Manabí province, hosting Liga Pro fixtures and providing the Ecuadorian Pacific coast with one of its larger stadiums.

## 8. Estadio Etho Vega Quezada, Loja

Capacity: 15,000. Loja's principal ground in southern Ecuador, in the highlands. Has hosted Liga Pro fixtures.

## 9. Estadio Bicentenario, Quito

Capacity: 10,000. Quito's smaller ground, used by Universidad Católica and other clubs in the city. The 2,800-metre altitude provides the same competitive characteristic as Casa Blanca and the Olímpico.

## 10. Estadio Modelo Alberto Spencer Herrera, Guayaquil

Capacity: 25,000. Guayaquil's older athletics-style stadium, named after the legendary Ecuadorian forward Alberto Spencer. Spencer scored more goals in Copa Libertadores history than any other player.

## Ecuadorian Liga Pro

Ecuador's Liga Pro Serie A is the top division of Ecuadorian football. Barcelona SC, Emelec, LDU Quito, and Independiente del Valle have dominated the modern era. The league produces Copa Libertadores knockout-round participants regularly, with LDU's 2008 Libertadores title and Independiente del Valle's two Copa Sudamericana wins (2019, 2022) the standout club-level achievements.

## The altitude factor

Ecuador's geographical advantage at altitude is so pronounced that CONMEBOL has occasionally debated whether grounds above 2,500 metres should be used for World Cup qualifying matches. The Olímpico Atahualpa and Casa Blanca at over 2,800 metres represent the strongest sustained altitude advantage in South American football outside La Paz, Bolivia. Coastal teams visiting Quito regularly struggle with the conditions, and the Ecuador national team's home record at altitude is the basis for the country's regular World Cup qualification.

## The Clásico del Astillero

The Clásico del Astillero between Barcelona SC and Emelec is one of South America's most-attended derbies. Both clubs share Guayaquil between them — Barcelona representing Ecuador's largest supporter base by some distance, Emelec representing the city's commercial and industrial heritage. The fixture has been played at both clubs' modern grounds. All stadiums are marked on the [map](/map).
