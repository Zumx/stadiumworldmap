A few stadiums are more than venues — they are landmarks, pilgrimage sites, and
symbols of a nation's relationship with sport. These are the icons.

## Maracanã, Rio de Janeiro

The spiritual home of Brazilian football, once holding nearly 200,000 — the
most mythologised stadium in the world.

## Camp Nou, Barcelona

Europe's largest stadium and the cathedral of FC Barcelona (undergoing a major
rebuild that will only enlarge its legend).

## Wembley Stadium, London

The arch-crowned home of English football, the stage every player dreams of.

## Old Trafford, Manchester

The "Theatre of Dreams," one of the most recognised club grounds on Earth.

## Santiago Bernabéu, Madrid

Real Madrid's reinvented fortress, now a futuristic retractable-roof
spectacle.

## Melbourne Cricket Ground, Australia

The "G" — a colossus of world cricket and Australian sport, a national
institution.

## Estadio Azteca, Mexico City

The only stadium to host two World Cup finals and the stage for the "Game of
the Century."

## San Siro, Milan

The spiralling-ramp giant shared by two clubs, one of football's most
atmospheric grounds.

## Beijing National Stadium ("Bird's Nest")

The 2008 Olympic centrepiece — architecture so distinctive it became the city's
emblem.

## La Bombonera, Buenos Aires

Boca Juniors' steep, shaking, deafening bowl — frequently called the most
intense atmosphere in world football.

## What makes a stadium iconic

It is rarely just size. It is a defining match (the Maracanã, the Azteca), an
unmistakable shape (the Bird's Nest, Wembley's arch), or an atmosphere that
became legend (La Bombonera, San Siro). Iconic stadiums turn architecture into
collective memory.

## Visiting the icons

Almost all run daily stadium tours and museums — the reliable way to stand on
these grounds without chasing scarce derby tickets. For a live match at the
biggest clubs, expect to need membership, pre-sale access, or hospitality
booked months ahead. Some, like the Camp Nou and Bernabéu, are mid-redevelopment
— check what is open before travelling.
