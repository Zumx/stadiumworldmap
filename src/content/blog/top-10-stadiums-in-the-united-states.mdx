American stadium culture is unmatched in scale and spectacle, from
century-old ballparks to NFL palaces. These ten are the ones to put on a
sports travel list.

## 1. Michigan Stadium ("The Big House"), Ann Arbor

The largest stadium in the United States, holding over 107,000 for college
football — a cathedral of the sport.

## 2. Fenway Park, Boston

The oldest Major League Baseball park (1912), home of the Green Monster — every
seat is a history lesson.

## 3. Wrigley Field, Chicago

Ivy-walled and beloved, the other great surviving classic ballpark.

## 4. Lambeau Field, Green Bay

The "Frozen Tundra" — the NFL's most storied cold-weather stadium and a
genuine pilgrimage.

## 5. SoFi Stadium, Los Angeles

A translucent-roofed architectural marvel and one of the most expensive
stadiums ever built.

## 6. Allegiant Stadium, Las Vegas

A black glass monolith on the Strip — futuristic NFL spectacle.

## 7. Rose Bowl, Pasadena

A historic bowl in the San Gabriel foothills, host of legendary games and a
World Cup final.

## 8. Notre Dame Stadium, Indiana

College football tradition distilled — "Touchdown Jesus" and all.

## 9. Oracle Park, San Francisco

A waterfront ballpark where home runs splash into the bay.

## 10. Mercedes-Benz Stadium, Atlanta

A retractable petal roof and a fan-friendly pricing model that reset
expectations.

## How to attend a US game

Tickets vary wildly by sport and seat; buy from the team or official
marketplace to avoid resale traps. College football game days are all-day
festivals — tailgating is the event as much as the game. NFL and MLB
single-game tickets are easier than playoff or rivalry dates, which spike
hard.

## Plan around the season

Each sport has its window: NFL and college football autumn into winter, MLB
April–October, with marquee dates (rivalry games, opening day, playoffs)
booking out months ahead. Pick the fixture first; the stadium follows.

## On-the-day tips

Arrive early for tailgating and to clear the security and parking crush. Bring
a clear bag (NFL/MLB clear-bag policies are strict), sun or cold gear for the
long day, and cash. Learn the basics of the sport and the rivalry — context
turns a game into an event.
