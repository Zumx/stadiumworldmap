Russia's stadium landscape combines monumental Soviet-era arenas with the
modern grounds built for the 2018 FIFA World Cup. These ten are the most
significant by history, scale, or design.

## 1. Luzhniki Stadium, Moscow

The national stadium and the country's largest — host of the 2018 World Cup
final and a 1980 Olympic centrepiece.

## 2. Krestovsky Stadium (Gazprom Arena), St. Petersburg

A spaceship-like arena with a retractable roof and sliding pitch, home of
Zenit.

## 3. VTB Arena (Dynamo), Moscow

A modern stadium rebuilt within the historic Dynamo footprint.

## 4. Otkritie Arena (Spartak Stadium), Moscow

Spartak Moscow's striking club ground, clad in scaled red-and-white panels.

## 5. Kazan Arena, Kazan

A landmark riverside stadium with one of Europe's largest exterior screens.

## 6. Rostov Arena, Rostov-on-Don

A flowing-roof World Cup stadium on the Don's left bank.

## 7. Mordovia Arena, Saransk

A vivid, sun-coloured 2018 venue and a symbol of regional investment.

## 8. Volgograd Arena, Volgograd

A lattice-façade stadium beneath the towering Motherland Calls monument.

## 9. Fisht Stadium, Sochi

Built for the 2014 Winter Olympics ceremonies, later a World Cup venue.

## 10. Ekaterinburg Arena, Yekaterinburg

Famous for its temporary external stands — an unusual engineering solution to
capacity rules.

## How stadium visits work

Several clubs and venues offer stadium tours and museums (Luzhniki, Gazprom
Arena, Spartak) outside matchdays. Matchday tickets for the Russian Premier
League are generally affordable; a Fan ID or equivalent identity registration
has historically been required for major events — check current entry rules
well ahead.

## Plan around the season and travel

The football season runs across the European calendar with a winter break;
spring and late summer fixtures offer the most comfortable weather given
Russia's climate range. These cities are far apart — group a realistic cluster
(Moscow and St. Petersburg) rather than crossing the country.

## On-the-day tips

Arrive early for security and identity checks, bring photo ID, dress for a wide
temperature range, and learn the club rivalries for context. Stadium tours are
the reliable option when matchday logistics are complex.

## A note on travel context

Travel conditions and access can change; check current guidance and entry
requirements before planning a trip built around live fixtures.
